Shiva Purānam

  • Shiva Purānam is one of the principal Purānas of the Hindu tradition, devoted to Lord Shiva.
  • It is counted among the Astādasa Mahāpurānas (the eighteen major Purānas) and occupies a central place in Shaiva tradition.
  • It explains on the understanding of Shiva Tattvam through bhakti, jnāna, dharma, and moksha.

Explore Shiva Purānam

  • Explore the structure of Shiva Purānam which consists of seven chapters namely Vidveswara, Rudra, Shata Rudra, Koti Rudra, Umaa, Kailasa and Vayaveeya Samhita.
  • The text elaborates on the glory, nature, and cosmic role of Lord Shiva, through narratives, dialogues, and symbolic teachings.
  • Shiva Purānam teaches seekers how to live a righteous life with faith, devotion, humility and gradually move closer to Lord Shiva.

Shiva Gita

  • Shiva Gita is a sacred text involves dialogue between Lord Shiva and Shri Rama.
  • During Ramayana period, After the abduction of Sita by Ravana, Shri Rama is advised by Agastya Maharshi to undertake the Pashupata Shiva Deeksha.
  • The Shiva Gita is the instruction given by Lord Shiva to Shri Rama in response to his questions.

Question
I want to understand Shiva Purānam, how can I do it easily?
Response (English)
To understand the Shiva Purānam easily, you can follow these steps:

1. **Start with the Overview**: Familiarize yourself with the general introduction of the Shiva Purānam to grasp the fundamental concepts related to Lord Shiva, his attributes, and his significance in the cosmos.

2. **Read the Chapters (Samhitas)**: The Shiva Purānam consists of several chapters, such as - Vidyeshwara Samhita - Rudra Samhita - Shata Rudra Samhita - Koṭi Rudra Samhita - Uma Samhita - Kailasa Samhita - Vayaviyya Samhita. Consider going through these chapters one at a time.

3. **Seek Guidance**: If possible, join a study group or find a teacher knowledgeable in Shiva Purānam who can provide explanations and insights.

Consider beginning with topics like the significance of Shiva in various aspects or the concept of devotion to Lord Shiva. Let me know if you have any specific area of interest or suggestion!
